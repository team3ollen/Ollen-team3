Dry and waste management systems are designed to handle different types of waste in an organized and eco-friendly way. Dry waste includes materials like plastic, paper, glass, cardboard, and metals that can be recycled or reused, while wet waste mainly consists of kitchen scraps, food leftovers, and garden waste that can be composted. In a proper waste management system, waste is first segregated at the source into dry and wet categories. Dry waste is collected, sorted, and sent for recycling or safe disposal, whereas wet waste is processed through composting or biogas plants to create useful products like manure or energy. This system not only reduces the burden on landfills but also helps conserve natural resources, lowers pollution, and promotes sustainable living.

Ultrasonic Sensor

ğŸ“ The ultrasonic sensor is used to measure the level of waste inside the bin. It sends ultrasonic waves and calculates the distance by receiving the echo. When the bin is nearly full, it can trigger a notification or alert the waste collection system. This ensures timely disposal and prevents overflowing bins in public spaces.

Moisture Sensor

ğŸ’§ The moisture sensor helps to differentiate between wet waste and dry waste. Wet waste such as food leftovers, fruits, and vegetable peels have higher moisture content, while plastics, paper, and metals remain dry. By sensing this difference, the system can automatically segregate the waste into the correct bin, improving recycling and composting efficiency.
